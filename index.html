Project Marquee

Wellcom internal project - ongoing

This was one of my largest and most complex projects: redesigning and modernizing the user interface of a legacy SaaS application to improve usability, address long-standing pain points, and align the product with current user expectations.

Discovery & Research

The project began with extensive user research to identify friction points and usability issues within the legacy platform. I conducted user interviews, gathered feedback from existing customers, and collaborated with internal support teams to collect recurring pain points and feature requests.

Journey Mapping & Workflow Analysis

Using insights from research, I mapped out current user journeys and workflows to understand how users navigated the system and where inefficiencies existed. This allowed me to pinpoint bottlenecks, redundant steps, and opportunities for streamlining task flows.

Collaborative Ideation & Early Design Exploration

Working closely with stakeholders, product managers, developers, and different user groups, we collaboratively defined project goals and prioritised features. I facilitated design workshops to align on new interaction patterns that would modernize the interface while preserving core functionality for existing users.

Low-Fidelity Prototyping & Information Architecture

I developed wireframes to explore updated layouts, navigation structures, and content hierarchies that better reflected users’ mental models. Particular focus was placed on improving task efficiency, reducing cognitive load, and ensuring consistency across complex workflows.

High-Fidelity Design & Prototyping

With validated wireframes, I produced high-fidelity UI designs that brought a modern, clean aesthetic to the platform, improving both usability and visual appeal. Interactive prototypes were created to simulate key workflows and support developer handoff.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

Throughout the project, I maintained close collaboration with developers to ensure technical feasibility and accurate implementation of design components. Regular reviews with stakeholders and user representatives ensured alignment across business and user needs.

User Validation & Iteration

Usability testing sessions were conducted during the design process. Low-fidelity prototypes were tested in internal teams to capture user feedback. This feedback loop informed multiple iterations, refining the interface to ensure the redesign addressed actual user pain points and improved task success rates.

Outcome

This is still an ongoing project. I am hoping that the redesigned SaaS platform successfully modernized the user experience, reduced user frustration, improved task efficiency, and positioned the product for future scalability — all while retaining familiarity for long-standing users transitioning from the legacy system.

Boardgame App

Academy Xi

This self-initiated project allowed me to explore and strengthen my UX practice by addressing a common challenge: reducing user reluctance to engage with rulebooks and complex rule systems, often encountered in board games, tabletop games, and similar experiences.

Discovery & Research

I started by conducting a competitive analysis to examine existing solutions and identify gaps in how rule content is currently presented. To better understand user needs and build empathy, I created personas representing both novice and experienced players, highlighting their unique frustrations, goals, and behaviors related to learning new rules.

Journey Mapping & Problem Framing

Using journey maps, I visualized the typical user experience when engaging with rulebooks. This helped pinpoint where users encountered friction, confusion, and drop-off moments — allowing me to define the key problem areas that required design intervention.

Quantitative & Qualitative Research

To complement my initial findings, I conducted a survey using Typeform to collect quantitative data from a broader audience. This provided insights into user habits, preferred learning formats, and pain points when engaging with rulebooks, giving me data-driven direction for feature prioritization.

Ideation & Low-Fidelity Prototyping

Drawing from research insights, I ideated potential solutions that would accommodate different learning styles and reduce cognitive load. Initial low-fidelity wireframes were created using Adobe XD, exploring different ways to structure, condense, and present rule information.

Design Execution & High-Fidelity Prototyping

The most promising design concepts were developed into high-fidelity, interactive prototypes using Axure. The solution evolved into an application-based database that offered multiple formats for rule consumption — including shortened text summaries, audio narration, and video tutorials — providing users with flexible ways to learn based on their preferences.

User Validation & Iteration

I conducted usability testing sessions with representative users to gather direct feedback on the prototype. Insights from testing led to several design refinements that improved clarity, usability, and overall engagement with the rule content.

Outcome

The final prototype successfully demonstrated a multi-format rule delivery system that simplifies rule learning, accommodates various user learning styles, and transforms a traditionally frustrating experience into an intuitive and approachable one.

Internal Tooling Improvement — JSON/YAML Form Template

Leading Financial Institution

This project focused on improving internal developer workflows by addressing recurring issues related to the creation and editing of JSON and YAML files — a task that, despite being routine, was prone to frequent errors.

Discovery & Problem Framing

Through observation and informal conversations, I identified a surprising pain point among developers: repeated typos, syntax errors, and formatting issues while manually creating or editing JSON and YAML files. While developers were familiar with the formats, the manual process was tedious and error-prone, especially under time pressure.

User Research & Journey Mapping

To better understand the workflow, I created user journey maps that captured how developers interacted with these files in their day-to-day tasks. I also developed user personas based on different levels of technical confidence and frequency of use.

Identifying Pain Points

By mapping out their processes, I was able to pinpoint where errors were occurring — most commonly during initial file creation and while making manual updates. These issues often led to delays, broken integrations, and unnecessary rework.

Solution Design

Based on the identified pain points, I designed a reusable form-based template to standardize JSON and YAML creation. The form guided developers through required fields, reduced the likelihood of typos, and automated consistent formatting.

Pilot & Rollout

I trialled the form with a small group of developers on the floor to gather early feedback and ensure it integrated smoothly into their existing workflows. Following a successful pilot, the template was adopted across the wider team.

Outcome

The solution significantly reduced formatting errors and improved overall efficiency in creating structured data files. It also increased developer confidence by removing repetitive, error-prone tasks and replacing them with a structured, guided process.

DET POC Web Application

DET

The Department of Education and Training engaged our team to design a proof of concept that addressed the enrolment experience for two key user groups: new students enrolling in courses, and administrators managing student records.

Discovery & Research

We began by conducting stakeholder interviews to better understand the goals, constraints, and existing pain points from both student and administrator perspectives. To further empathize with users, we created personas representing typical students and administrators, mapping out their needs, motivations, and frustrations.

Journey Mapping & Problem Framing

Next, we developed user journey maps to visualize the end-to-end enrolment process for both user groups. This helped identify friction points, opportunities for streamlining, and areas where the digital experience could better support users.

Rapid Ideation & Low-Fidelity Prototyping

With a limited timeline of under two months, we facilitated design workshops to generate initial concepts quickly. Low-fidelity wireframes were produced to explore multiple interaction patterns, focusing on simplicity, clarity, and minimizing cognitive load — particularly for first-time student users.

Design Execution & High-Fidelity Prototyping

Once wireframes were validated, I translated them into high-fidelity interactive prototypes. The student experience prioritized clear guidance through enrolment steps, while the administrator interface allowed easy creation, editing, and deletion of student records. Accessibility and consistency with government design standards were also key considerations.

Outcome

The final proof of concept successfully demonstrated an intuitive, dual-user enrolment system that balanced administrative control with a guided, supportive student experience — all delivered within the tight timeframe.

eBlueprint Website

eBlueprint

The client was undergoing a full brand refresh, redefining their business values and visual identity. As part of this transformation, they required a complete redesign of their corporate website to better reflect their updated brand direction.

Discovery & Planning

The project began with collaborative workshops to align on the client’s new brand values, goals, and vision for the website. We worked closely with stakeholders to understand business requirements, content priorities, and desired user behaviors.

UX Design & Information Architecture

With a focus on simplicity and ease of navigation, we mapped out the core information architecture to ensure intuitive content flow and user journeys. The goal was to create a design that would minimize complexity both for end users and during development.

Wireframing & Visual Design

Working alongside another designer, wireframes were created to outline page structures and content hierarchy. High-fidelity mock-ups were developed in Adobe Photoshop, translating the brand’s new look and feel into a clean, modern interface.

Prototyping & User Flow Visualization

Using Adobe XD, we built interactive prototypes to demonstrate user flows and page interactions, providing stakeholders and developers with a clear visualization of the end-to-end experience.

Front-End Development & Collaboration

In my role as front-end developer, I collaborated closely with the development team to translate the designs into a responsive, accessible website built on the Drupal CMS framework. Design decisions were made to ensure ease of content management and scalability for future updates.

Ongoing Maintenance & Enhancements

Post-launch, I continue to maintain the website, implementing ongoing enhancements, optimizing code, and updating styles as business needs evolve.

Outcome

The redesigned website successfully reflects the client’s new brand identity, offers a simplified user experience, and supports long-term flexibility for content and design updates.
